Workgroup

In computer networking a work group is collection of computers connected on a LAN that share the common resources and responsibilities. Workgroup is Microsoft's term for a peer-to-peer local area network Computers running Microsoft operating systems in the same work group may share files, printers, or Internet connection.[1] Work group contrasts with a domain, in which computers rely on centralized authentication.

If you use multiple computers in your business you can assign them to multiple workgroups. You can join existing workgroups on your network at any time and organize your computers as you see fit. Before you can view other workgroups and connect to their computers in your business network, you must configure Windows 7 and enable the network discovery feature.

1.

Click "Control Panel" in the Start menu to open the Control Panel.

2.

Click the "Network and Internet" link to navigate to the Network and Internet section of the Control Panel.

3.

Click the "Network and Sharing Center" link.

4.

Click the "Change advanced sharing settings" link in the left pane to open the Advanced Sharing Settings page.

5.

Click the "Turn on network discovery" radio button in the Network Discovery section

6.

Click the "Save Changes" button to apply and save the new settings.

7.

Click the "Back" button in the top left corner of the Control Panel to return to the previous section.

8.

Click the "View network computers and devices" link in the Network and Sharing Center section to view the computers and workgroups in your network.
